Special sessions

Big Data for Urban and Regional Analysis

AAG Annual Meeting

San Francisco, CA
March 29-April 2, 2016

  • Call
  • Conference

The landscape of urban and regional geographic research is evolving as a result of the vast integration of digital technologies into nearly every aspect of human activity. The streams of data resulting from social media, open government initiatives, the pervasive presence of sensors, and many other sources collectively form what has now been termed Big Data. Of special interest to geographers is that these data tend to be geo-referenced, which provides a potentially useful data source for studying cities and regions. By connecting this data availability with computational advances that enable processing and modeling at a very low cost, the idea of building a science around spatial big data becomes attainable and almost imperative. The derived question from the current situation has to do with the value added of the growing data wealth in spatial sciences. What can we learn about cities and regions by employing Big Data and other non-traditional data sources in urban and regional modeling? In order to answer this question we call for papers focusing on the following points:

  • Empirical applications of Big Data in quantitative geography
  • Studies bridging the gap between traditional sources of (small) data (e.g., censuses) and new sources of (big) data
  • Applications and experiences in which Big Data is used to inform policy decisions
  • Studies that quantitatively explore the strengths and weaknesses of Big Data
